80% Diet - 20% Routine


Whilst not necessarily accurate, it goes without saying that one without the other will keep your goals out of reach. Without a proper routine, it is impossible to build strength, muscle and improve performance, whilst neglecting a proper diet will render said workouts, pointless.


So what is a proper diet?

A proper diet should be tailored to suit your routine and goals. It is ridiculous trying to build muscle, for example, on a calorie deficit; to build muscle you need extra fuel. More generally, you should aim to eat enough calories to suit your lifestyle and activity requirements, plus or minus 150-500 calories depending on whether you want to gain or lose weight and at what rate - obviously if you want to maintain your weight then you eat to the initial figure. Second to this are your macros - how much carbohydrate, fat and protein makes up your diet. Endurance sports and weight gain regimes typically require more carbohydrate for your body to convert into energy, whilst protein plays a bigger part in strength and recovery routines, to maximise muscle repair and growth.

The more abstract factor in dieting is body-types, which people will argue exist or not; but we've all seen the tall skinny guys and the short-stocky (dwarven) guys. There are 3 main body types, but here at Aesthetics we feel things aren't so black-and-white, and that these body types may have a sort of in-between body-types where they crossover.


The Body Types:

  • Ectomorph: This is your typical, tall, slender frame - narrow shoulders, small bone structure...and can eat the entire KFC and it's staff and still lose weight! It's easy for ectomorphs to lose weight but they struggle to put it on, so their calorie requirement is usually much higher.

  • Mesomorph: Mesomorphs are the chosen few - the genetically gifted - that have the ideal medium between height and bone structure. Usually they can lose and gain weight as required, and are considered to have the "best" frame for building muscle. As far as calorific requirements go, mesomorphs don't have to starve or stuff their face and can generally eat quite moderately.

  • Endomorph: The endomorph population are those you hear complaining (usually directed at the ectomorphs) that even looking at a burger piles on the pounds. By definition, they usually have shorter bone structures, meaning shorter arms, torsos and (sometimes) overall height. All this makes for a particularly strong individual, with a wide frame for building muscle - thereby granting the illusion of size. It isn't all gravy though - endomorphs really do struggle with their weight and are generally more receptive to carbohydrates.

  • Meso-Endo: This isn't strictly a body type, but we apply it to individuals who are a little more susceptible to weight gain with the body structure of a mesomorph.

  • Meso-Ecto: Likewise, meso-ecto describes those who aren't necessarily tall and skinny, but do have a hard time gaining weight.
